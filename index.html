Research overview

Evaluation of Explainable AI for Time Series

My PhD research focuses on evaluating explanations for time-series AI systems. This page collects work on perturbation metrics, ground-truth evaluation, and human understanding.

Perturbation metrics

Class-dependent evaluation effects

Perturbation analysis is often used to evaluate whether feature attributions are correct. These papers study when that evaluation depends on the predicted class, and what that means for trusting the scores.

Paper 1

Class-dependent perturbation effects in evaluating time-series attributions

Takeaway: perturbation-based scores can look strong for one class and weak for another, even within the same dataset and model.

We evaluate time-series attribution methods across datasets, model architectures, and perturbation strategies. The results suggest that class-aware reporting is needed, because aggregate scores can hide systematic differences between classes.

Baer, G., Grau, I., Zhang, C., & Van Gorp, P. (2025). Class-Dependent Perturbation Effects in Evaluating Time Series Attributions. Explainable Artificial Intelligence, 292-314.

DOI Open version

Paper 2

Why do class-dependent evaluation effects occur?

Takeaway: class-dependent effects can arise from simple differences in synthetic features, and perturbation scores may disagree with ground-truth metrics.

We use controlled synthetic time series where the relevant feature locations are known. This lets us compare perturbation-based degradation scores with ground-truth evaluation and study when the two views of attribution quality diverge.

Baer, G., Grau, I., Zhang, C., & Van Gorp, P. (2026). Why do Class-Dependent Evaluation Effects Occur with Time Series Feature Attributions? Machine Learning and Principles and Practice of Knowledge Discovery in Databases, 380-395.

DOI Open version

Human outcomes

Metrics and human understanding

A computational score is useful only if we know what it tells us. This project studies how explanation correctness relates to whether people can understand an AI decision pattern.

User study

Does explanation correctness matter?

Takeaway: lower correctness can reduce human understanding, but the relationship is not a smooth one-to-one mapping.

Participants used explanations to predict an AI system's decisions in a time-series task. The results suggest that functional metrics should be validated against human outcomes, because better scores do not always translate into equally better understanding.

Baer, G., Zhang, C., Grau, I., & Van Gorp, P. (2026). Does Explanation Correctness Matter? Linking Computational XAI Evaluation to Human Understanding. arXiv:2603.25251.

DOI arXiv

Synthetic data

Tools for controlled evaluation

xaitimesynth is a small package for synthetic time-series XAI evaluation. It creates datasets with known ground truth so attribution methods can be tested in controlled settings.

Package

Ground-truth based evaluation for time-series explanations

Takeaway: synthetic data can make evaluation assumptions visible, because the relevant time points are known by design.

The package provides dataset generation tools, examples, and documentation for evaluating feature attributions on synthetic time series. It is useful when you want a controlled setting before moving to messier real-world data.
